I The Shop

A little larger than most
people realize.

The room is deceptive. From Highway 111 it looks like a single bay; inside, it opens into fourteen thousand square feet of golf clubs, racquets, paddles, shoes, and apparel — over a dozen departments stocked with more than a hundred name brands. Callaway and Titleist and Ping on one side, Babolat and Head and Yonex on the other. A footwear wall that runs the length of the back. A bench where racquets are strung and clubs are re-gripped while you wait.

Pete Carlson learned the trade in Oakland and followed his mentor Don Sherwood to Palm Desert at the very end of the 1970s. A year later he opened his own store a few doors down. That store has not moved since.

"At Pete's we understand that to some, golf is a game — and to others, it's a way of life."

— Pete Carlson

III The Bench Work

What the big boxes
contract out, we do in-house.

The most honest part of the store is the back room. Shaft replacement, loft and lie adjustments, grip swaps, shaft extensions. Certified racquet stringing with quick turnaround. Grommet replacement, customization, balance. The bench is where 45 years of relationships turn into trust you can actually feel.

IV Community

More than a store.
A desert institution.

Each February · Since c. 2006

The Pete Carlson Golf Expo.

Twenty-plus manufacturers. Roughly four thousand attendees each year. Al Geiberger — Mr. 59, the first man to shoot 59 on the PGA Tour — has been a fixture. Free and open to the desert. Held at the College of the Desert Golf Center driving range, 73450 Fred Waring Drive.

On the back patio · Since 2010

Jazz for Jazz Lovers.

A concert series in partnership with the American Jazz Institute (a 501(c)(3)), running in the back of the shop for over a decade. Proceeds support music education and scholarships for young musicians in the Coachella Valley.
