## Flagged for Zack — unverified or sourced from research only

- **"Only full-line golf, tennis, and pickleball shop in the Coachella Valley"** — comes from the research competitive analysis (April 2026 scan), not the site itself. Research labels it "defensible from competitive scan: no dedicated tennis or pickleball specialty retailer operates in the valley; PGA TSS carries racquet sports only as secondary departments." Strong claim to make but should be confirmed by Zack/Pete before going live.
- **"~2,500 clubs in inventory on any given day"** (Direction 1, Golf section) — from research synopsis, not from the site's own copy. Confirm with Pete or remove if uncertain.
- **"Between Portola and San Luis Rey"** (both directions, Visit section) — placement detail added for flavor. 73-741 Hwy 111 falls on that stretch based on a Google Maps read, but worth confirming with Pete that those are the right cross-street references. Easy to change.
- **Expo founding year c. 2006 + Al Geiberger appearances + Barney Adams co-origination** — all from the research document, not on the site. The site's golf-expo-2020 page says "for the last 15 years" (suggesting ~2005 founding) but doesn't name Geiberger or Adams. Research calls these "well-documented" but from external profiles (Palm Springs Life, Desert Sun). Safe to use but good to confirm with Pete.
- **"BNP Paribas Open" / Indian Wells references** — research confirms the shop is "the de-facto resource" during tournament week (documented in Racquet Sports Industry 2010, Wilson Tennis 2012). **No formal partnership or "official stringer" claim is made in the mockup copy** — language stays informal ("de-facto resource during BNP Paribas Open week"). Still worth Zack confirming it reads right.
- **"Since c. 2006" on the Expo** — using "c." (circa) as a hedge. Research infers this from the Expo's current numbering (20th annual in 2025). Confirm with Pete for the exact founding year.
